Side A · 1,797,006 games · Lichess · Elo 1000–1599

White wins. Always. The first-move advantage is real.

In 1.8 million chess games at the level where most people play, White wins about 50 of every 100 games and Black wins only 46. Three-point-nine percentage points doesn't sound like much. Stretched across 6 million games, it's tens of thousands of decided games — and the gap is visible at every cut we tested.

It holds at every skill level.

If first-move advantage were just beginner luck, we'd see the gap shrink as players improved.

It doesn't. White wins more at Elo 1000, more at 1200, and more at 1400. The line climbs slightly from +2.5pp to +3.9pp — preparation actually magnifies the advantage as players learn theory.

It survives any time control.

Maybe the advantage only shows up in slow games where preparation matters? No. We tested across bullet, blitz, rapid, and classical.

White wins more in every bucket. Rapid actually shows the biggest gap (+4.7pp), and even bullet — where everyone is just slamming pieces — gives White +3.4pp. You can't escape it by changing the clock.

White's strongest weapons.

Some openings make the first-move advantage explode. Sharp tactical openings — Bishop's Opening, Italian Game, Philidor Defense, Ruy Lopez — all give White over 52% wins.

The Bishop's Opening alone hits 54.4%. That's a 12pp gap over Black. If you want White's edge to be undeniable, this is where it lives.
