Side B · 1,797,006 games · Lichess · Elo 1000–1599

Color is just noise. The real game is everywhere else.

Yes, White wins +3.9pp more games than Black. But your opening choice swings results five times harder, and your rating relative to your opponent swings them sixteen times harder. The first-move advantage is a measurable signal — and the smallest one in chess.

Rating gap matters 16× more than color.

Here is the most damning chart for "color is destiny."

When Black is rated 200 or more points higher than White, Black wins about 80% of the time. When White is rated 200+ higher, White wins about 80% — symmetric mirror. Color contributes +3.9pp; rating contributes up to +30pp.

If you want to predict the winner, the rating gap dwarfs which colour they got.

The bottom line

Color is real. Color is the smallest factor in the game.
